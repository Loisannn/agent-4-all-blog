# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo developer/tech blogger writing technical posts. Comfortable with Markdown, values speed and precision. Writes in a focused environment — likely from a terminal, editor, or dashboard, not a casual browsing context. The public audience is other developers reading technical content.

## Product Purpose

A blog CMS that serves two jobs equally: fast, frictionless writing and publishing (the admin experience), and a polished, trustworthy reading experience (the public blog). Success means the writer forgets the tool exists while writing, and the reader trusts the content enough to stay and read.

## Brand Personality

**Technical, precise, authoritative.** The voice is confident without being loud. It trusts the user's competence — no hand-holding, no cheerfulness, no filler. The visual language communicates "built by and for developers" through precision, not through raw or brutalist aesthetics. Think Stripe's polish, PlanetScale's depth, Railway's confidence — developer-native tools that feel crafted, not sterile.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS templates**: No gradient-purple hero sections, no dashboard-y card grids, no stock-illustration vibes, no "hero-metric" stat blocks.
- **Over-designed agency work**: No scroll-jacking, no parallax everything, no glassmorphism as default, no decorative mega-animations.
- **Brutalist or raw HTML**: Not intentionally unstyled. The polish is real, not ironic.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through restraint.** The tool stays out of the way. Every element earns its place; decoration that doesn't serve clarity is removed. Like a well-tuned terminal or a sharp text editor, confidence comes from what's absent.

2. **Developer-native, never developer-only.** Technical precision in every interaction without alienating anyone. The CMS is fast and keyboard-friendly; the public blog is polished and readable. Both audiences are respected.

3. **Depth without clutter.** Visual hierarchy through weight, spacing, and controlled color — not through more elements. Richness comes from texture (subtle borders, thoughtful shadows, precise typography), not from decoration.

4. **Consistency as velocity.** The same button shape, the same form vocabulary, the same state language across admin and public surfaces. Users build muscle memory; the interface doesn't surprise them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Keyboard-navigable admin. Focus indicators visible and consistent. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color is never the sole indicator of state.
